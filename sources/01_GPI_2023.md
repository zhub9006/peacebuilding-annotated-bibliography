## 📊 Key Data Extracted

### Global Headline Findings (GPI 2023)
- **Global average GPI score** deteriorated by **0.42%** in 2023 — the **9th consecutive annual deterioration**
- **84 countries** recorded improvement; **79 countries** recorded deterioration (deteriorations were larger)
- **16 of 23** GPI indicators worsened in 2023; only 7 improved
- **Conflict deaths** increased by **96%** to **238,000** in 2023 (from ~121,000 in 2022)
- Ethiopia recorded **more conflict deaths than Ukraine** in 2023, eclipsing the previous global peak during the Syrian war
- **79 countries** witnessed increased levels of conflict including Ethiopia, Myanmar, Ukraine, Israel, and South Africa

### Regional GPI Scores (2023)
| Region | Avg. GPI Score | Rank (most→least peaceful) |
|---|---|---|
| Europe | **1.702** | 1st (most peaceful) |
| North America | ~2.057 | 3rd |
| Asia-Pacific | ~2.042 | 2nd |
| Latin America & Caribbean | ~2.130 | 4th |
| **Sub-Saharan Africa** | **2.163** | 5th |
| South Asia | ~2.418 | 6th |
| Russia & Eurasia | ~2.680 | 7th |
| **MENA** | **3.409** | 8th (least peaceful) |

### Notable Country-Level Changes
- **Largest improvement:** Libya (2nd consecutive year), followed by Burundi, Oman, Côte d'Ivoire, Afghanistan
- **Largest deteriorations:** Ukraine (largest drop), Russia (5th largest drop)
- Europe remains most peaceful region; home to **7 of 10** most peaceful countries
- The other 3 most peaceful countries are in Asia-Pacific

## 🗒️ Annotation

The GPI is the most widely cited composite peace index globally. Its three-domain framework captures structural dimensions of peace beyond mere fatality counts, making it the standard benchmark for regional peace comparisons.

**Strengths:**
- Comprehensive 23-indicator methodology covering safety, conflict, and militarisation
- 163-country coverage with 15+ year time series for trend analysis
- Transparent methodology and free data access
- Companion *Positive Peace Index (PPI)* available for structural peace analysis

**Limitations:**
- Annual snapshot; does not capture within-year volatility
- Composite score aggregation may obscure domain-specific variation (e.g., a country peaceful domestically but highly militarised)
- GPI scores are relative rankings, not absolute measures of violence intensity
- The 96% jump in conflict deaths (to 238,000) reflects both genuine escalation and improved data collection methods

**Relevance to research question:**
The GPI regional averages provide the primary dependent variable for comparing peacefulness between high-investment (SSA, MENA) and low-investment (Europe, North America) regions. The 1.707-point differential between MENA (3.409) and Europe (1.702) is the largest cross-regional GPI gap documented in this research.
