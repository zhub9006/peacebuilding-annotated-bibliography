## 📊 Key Data Extracted

### 2023 Global Conflict Snapshot
| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| State-based conflicts recorded | **59** (in 34 countries) |
| Highest since | **1946** — record high |
| Battle-related deaths (state-based) | **>122,000** |
| Year rank by violence | **3rd most violent** since end of Cold War |
| Primary death contributors | Ukraine and Gaza |
| Non-state conflicts | **75** (~21,000 deaths) |
| Non-state deaths avg per conflict | **280 deaths/conflict** *(calculated: 21,000 ÷ 75)* |
| Countries with one-sided violence | **35** |

### Regional Highlights
- **Americas:** Highest number of non-state conflicts globally
- **Mexico:** ~14,000 non-state battle-related deaths (≈67% of global non-state total)
- **Sub-Saharan Africa:** Major contributors include Ethiopia, Sudan, DRC, Somalia, Mali
- **Europe:** Ukraine conflict is primary driver of European state-based deaths
- **Asia-Pacific:** Myanmar conflict intensified significantly

### Trend Analysis (1946–2023)
- 2023 marks a **YoY decrease** in battle-related deaths from 2022's spike (driven by Ukraine opening year)
- Despite decrease, 2023 is **3rd most violent post-Cold War year**
- Non-state conflict deaths **decreased** compared to prior years
- Non-state actors are primary drivers of one-sided violence fatalities
- **13 governments** responsible for one-sided violence against civilians in 2023

## 🗒️ Annotation

This PRIO Paper is the definitive annual synthesis of UCDP conflict data. Rustad provides both descriptive statistics and contextual analysis, making it invaluable for understanding the 2023 conflict landscape in historical perspective.

**Strengths:**
- Draws directly on UCDP — the gold standard for conflict fatality data
- Long time series (1946–2023) enables trend analysis
- Distinguishes state-based, non-state, and one-sided violence categories
- Authored by PRIO's Research Director, ensuring methodological rigor
- Open access

**Limitations:**
- UCDP data has inherent reporting lags; 2023 figures may be revised upward as more sources are incorporated
- "Battle-related deaths" definition excludes indirect deaths from disease, famine, displacement
- Regional share breakdowns require cross-referencing with UCDP GED dataset for precision
- The decrease from 2022 to 2023 in battle deaths is partly an artifact of data maturation for 2022

**Relevance to research question:**
Provides the primary fatality numerators for per-capita calculations. The 122,000 state-based deaths and 21,000 non-state deaths are the basis for all regional fatality rate estimates in `computed_metrics.md`. The finding that Mexico alone accounts for ~67% of non-state deaths highlights the importance of distinguishing conflict types when comparing regions.
