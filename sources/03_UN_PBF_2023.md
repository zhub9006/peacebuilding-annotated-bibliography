### PBF Mandate and Structure
- The PBF is the UN's **"financial instrument of first resort"** for sustaining peace in countries at risk of or affected by violent conflict
- Invests with UN entities, governments, regional organizations, multilateral banks, national MDTFs, and CSOs
- Works **across pillars** (peace + development + humanitarian + human rights)
- **Not a development fund** — specifically targets the peacebuilding gap between humanitarian and development funding

## 🗒️ Annotation

The annual SG Report on the PBF is the definitive official source for UN peacebuilding expenditure data. It provides country-level approval figures and implementing partner information, enabling regional aggregation.

**Strengths:**
- Official UN audited figures — highest reliability for funding data
- Country-level granularity allows custom regional aggregation
- Covers the full spectrum of PBF activities including gender-responsive peacebuilding
- Investment Map provides geographic visualization of allocations

**Limitations:**
- PBF represents only a fraction of total peacebuilding investment globally (bilateral ODA, World Bank, regional development banks add substantially more)
- $202M is a small figure relative to global military spending ($2,443 billion in 2023 per SIPRI — a ratio of roughly 1:12,000)
- Approved ≠ disbursed; actual spending may differ
- "Peacebuilding" is broadly defined and includes some activities that overlap with development or governance

**Relevance to research question:**
The PBF is the most precisely measurable proxy for dedicated peacebuilding investment at the UN level. The regional breakdown ($91.1M to Africa, $40.5M to MENA) forms the "investment" variable in the high vs. low investment comparison. The $5.6M average per country illustrates the relatively modest scale of dedicated peacebuilding resources relative to conflict intensity.

**Critical observation:**
Africa receives ~45% of PBF funds (~$91M) yet still has battle-death rates of ~51.6 per million — while Europe, receiving near-zero PBF funds, has ~16.3 per million. This apparent paradox is explained by the **endogeneity of investment**: funding flows to the most conflict-affected states, not the most peaceful ones.
