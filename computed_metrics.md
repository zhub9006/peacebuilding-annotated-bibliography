### 3.1 High-Investment vs. Low-Investment Group Comparison

**Group A — HIGH Peacebuilding Investment (SSA + MENA + South Asia):**
```
Combined estimated deaths:   ~103,700

                           = ~54.6 per million
```

```
Combined estimated deaths:   ~12,200 (primarily Ukraine)
```

**→ Fatality Rate Ratio (High/Low Investment Groups): 54.6 / 10.2 ≈ 5.35×**  
Regions receiving high peacebuilding investment experience conflict fatality rates approximately **5.35 times higher** than low-investment regions — a reflection of the fact that investment follows need, not the reverse.

## 7. Interpretation Notes

1. **Investment follows conflict, not peace:** The positive correlation between high PBF investment and high fatality rates is expected and by design — the PBF targets the most conflict-affected states.

2. **GPI scores capture more than deaths:** The GPI includes militarisation, societal safety, and structural violence indicators. MENA's extreme score (3.409) reflects both active conflict and high militarisation.

3. **Counterfactual question:** The relevant research question is not "do high-investment regions have fewer deaths?" but "would deaths be even higher *without* peacebuilding investment?" This requires longitudinal panel analysis (see UCDP/PRIO datasets).

4. **Data vintage:** GPI 2023 scores reflect the state of peace as of early 2023. PRIO 2024 paper covers full-year 2023 deaths. These are slightly misaligned temporally.

5. **Non-state violence under-counted:** UCDP battle-related deaths exclude disease and famine deaths caused by conflict. IEP's 238,000 figure is broader and more comprehensive.
